My dad was always very good with being cool under fire, and I like to think I got a bit of his talent. I always had a pretty loose leash as a child — my dad would only step in at the last possible moment, right when I was way in over my head. This freedom let me explore a lot, but there were definitely some times where he had to bail me out, and if he hadn't been cool under fire, I probably would've panicked and been unable to follow his direction on how to fix things.
A good example would be when he let me drive our boat around at age 5. I got to run the boat going through a bay, but when we got near a harbor, I nearly put our boats on the rocks. I always knew whenever I had messed up because dad's instructions would go from casual to very, very clear. In this case, it was no longer "Try taking the boat back that way;" it was "Ok Tapley, you need to put the boat all the way in reverse right now. Now turn the handle to the left two times and then straighten it out." There have been some times where I've needed to give clear instructions under pressure, and I think I handled it decently.
