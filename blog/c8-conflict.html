U3.W8: Conflict

Apr. 19, 2015

Prompt: Discuss a time you had a conflict. What was it about? What happened?

I did some house cleaning a while back, and in the process, I downloaded a lot of music CDs onto my parent's computer, uploaded the songs onto an iPod, and put them into a pile to be thrown out. My dad let the CDs go into the trash, but it was obvious he wasn't pleased.

What was the source of the conflict?

The question was whether to keep the CDs or not. I wanted to throw them out to reduce clutter in the house. He was worried the computer could crash and he'd lose all his music. I had the computer backed up on a hard drive, but it was obvious he still wanted to have the physical copies.

What basic emotions did you feel upon analyzing the conflict?

I was pretty frustrated. I can understand apprehension towards new technology, but MP3s are well enough established by now that I didn't see the point of having CD's randomly dispersed around our home office.

What actions did you take to resolve your conflict?

I tried to explain how the CDs were backed up onto the computer, the iPod, and the hard drive that contained the computer's backups. This helped, but I don't think it entirely resolved the problem. It was obvious he was still pretty bothered.

If you could go back, what would you do differently, if anything?

I would've slowed the process down. It's not that my dad's a luddite â€” he's actually pretty comfortable with technology. It's clear that the problem was in the way I presented the situation to him: "This is happening, unless you want to stop it" instead of "Do you want this to happen?"

What did you learn from this experience?

I didn't think my dad would take offense at what I was doing, since I hadn't thrown out his CDs, but I clearly judged it wrong. By placing them in a pile designated for the trash, I was putting the burden on him to keep the CDs out of the trash, which wasn't fair to him.
